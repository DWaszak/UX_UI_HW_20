Department of Treasury Redesign Case Study

In this research project, I planned and conducted 5 user tests with potential website visitors, performed a 34-point heuristic evaluation of select webpages, conducted 3 user card sorts of information architecture, and applied key findings to the redesign.

My Role & Responsibilities

User Researcher | Information Architect | User Interface Designer During this project I was responsible for conducting user tests on current website as well as redesigned prototype, evaluation and restructuring of website information architecture, & user interface design.

Timeline

4 Weeks

Research

We started with a thorough evaluation of the current website’s layout, navigation, content hierarchy, font usage, and really anything that we felt may affect a user’s experience. During this process we discovered that a large portion of this website is dedicated to linking to affiliated government agencies such as the IRS & U.S. Mint. This practice seems to add to the confusion of the Department of Treasury’s role and purpose of this website.

I planned and conducted 5 user tests with potential website visitors, performed a 34-point heuristic evaluation of select webpages, conducted 3 user card sorts of information architecture, and applied key findings to the redesign.

Key findings from our user research included:

-Confusion about the Department of Treasury’s role in our government

-Overwhelming amount of pages contained in navigation menus

-Shock by the dated appearance of certain web pages

-Frustration from conflicting information presented in the copy

-Inconsistent button, link, and navigation styles

The Problem

Although recently redesigned, the Department of Treasury website is full of jargon and excessive navigation, causing users to feel overwhelmed and stressed while attempting to navigate their way through the website.

Goal

How might we simplify and streamline navigation while expanding the modern UI elements used on the homepage to others pages within the website.

Ideation

We developed a user persona based on the ideal candidate to visit the pages of our user task flow and the pain points experienced during our user tests.

We then added notes from evaluations and user tests into a 2X2 matrix to help gauge the importance of our findings and what to address during our design process.

Through several rounds of card sorting we were able to get a better grasp of how users expected the information to be organized on the website. We took that information and created a new sitemap to guide the redesign of the website’s navigation.

Usability & Testing

After adding visual elements to all pages we performed another round of user tests to get feedback on our design choices. Received useful feedback about information hierarchy in the mobile version of the property details page, so once again iterated on the design of those pages. All in all I feel like the Auctions section was drastically improved.
