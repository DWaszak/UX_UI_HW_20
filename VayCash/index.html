Travel App Case Study

The goal of this assignment was to conceptualize and design mobile app to help the modern traveler based on findings through user research. Through that research I developed VayCash, an application to help the modern traveler set aside money for travel while gaining a better understanding of how their travel budget is allocated.

MY ROLE & RESPONSIBILITIES

User Researcher | User Interface Designer

TIMELINE

4 Weeks

Research

To begin researching this project, I started with a quantitative online survey to get an idea of how often the average person travels, the reason, how much they typically aim to spend, and what their overall goals are while travelling.

From there I wanted to dive deeper into the “hows and whys” with several qualitative 1-on-1 interviews, to truly get to know these potential users on a personal and emotional level.

My partner and I used affinity diagramming to find trends forming amongst the interview notes.

After condensing the affinity diagram we began moving these trending thoughts and feelings into an empathy map.

Through this empathy map we were able to gain insights into who our users may be and what forces they gain from as well as potential pains.

Key Findings:

  • No one seems to travel as often as they’d like, typically due to time and money constraints.
  • People don’t typically set a budget and then adhere to that while traveling.
  • Travelers often spend far more than expected during their travels.
  • Most travelers are seeking exciting and memorable experiences over luxurious accommodations

How might we help our users achieve their budget goals and help them better understand how their budget is being allocated in order to prevent them from spending more than expected?

The Problem

How often do you travel? The response I hear the most is “Not as often as I’d like to”. Travel can be expensive, and it’s tough to set money aside amidst our busy lives.

The Solution

Through creative savings techniques and calculated allocation of a travel budget, one can travel far more often than they ever thought possible.
